## Study 6: Use of generative artificial intelligence (AI) in psychiatry and mental health care: a systematic review.
**Authors:** Sara Kolding; Robert M Lundin; Lasse Hansen; S. Østergaard
**Year:** 2024.0
**Venue:** Acta Neuropsychiatrica
**Generative AI Technology Used:** Generative AI, ChatGPT

### How Generative AI is Used:
- OBJECTIVES
Tools based on generative artificial intelligence (AI) such as ChatGPT have the potential to transform modern society, including the field of medicine.
- Therefore, the aim of this study was to systematically review the literature on generative AI applications in psychiatry and mental health.
- CONCLUSIONS
Generative AI in psychiatry and mental health is a nascent but quickly expanding field.
- The literature mainly focuses on applications of ChatGPT, and finds that generative AI performs well, but notes that it is limited by significant safety and ethical concerns.
- Most studies focused on models that generate language, ChatGPT in particular.

### Key Findings:

1. **Application Areas**: Generative AI technologies are being explored in various aspects of mental health care, including diagnostic support, treatment recommendations, patient monitoring, and therapeutic interventions.

2. **Research Stage**: Many studies appear to be in early stages, with some focusing on systematic reviews of existing applications, while others explore potential uses through prompt experiments and pilot studies.

3. **Challenges Identified**: Several studies highlight important considerations including ethical concerns, privacy issues, algorithmic bias, and the need for human oversight in AI-assisted mental health care.

4. **Future Directions**: Research suggests that Generative AI holds promise for improving access to mental health services, personalizing treatments, and supporting clinicians, but requires careful implementation and validation.
